The Kiraida Munitions KM-40. Rugged, robust, and pretty inexpensive compared to other nations use fighter jets, these fighters are one that had proven their own worth during the Nusian-Mesonian War.

Armed with 6 7.62mm machine guns in the early production variant, she has some average firepower, though in the intermediate successor models they are armed with 4 12.7mm machine guns.

Being rugged and robust, she is also heavily modular in nature, mostly built to be easily maintained
izumo4402/20/24
The Tachikawa Metalworks D4B "Iskandar". Modern, reliable, and more often than not, a daredevil of itself, the Iskandar entered service for the KRNAF in December of 1940. Enough time to help pilots, both veterans and cadets, learn to familiarise with the new plane.

Unlike the KM-40, the D4B Iskandar has a simple yet innovative "cheese-grate" flaps which can theoretically allow the planes to go on a steep 60° dive without damaging the mechanisms within the flaps.

Her double-row 18-cylinder 910hp supercharged engine can push the aircraft to 410km/hr and is capable of carrying 500lbs of payload
