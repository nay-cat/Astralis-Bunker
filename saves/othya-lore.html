The currency of Othya is called the Othyan Tricone, it is backed by gold and gold is sold at a fixed rate of 50 Othyan Tricones per ounce, there are banknotes for 10 Othyan Tricones, 20 Othyan Tricones, 50 Othyan Tricones, 100 Othyan Tricones, 200 Othyan Tricones, and the rarely used 500 Othyan Tricones.

Alongside this, Othya uses silver coins, those being 1 Othyan Byn, 2 Othyan Byn, 5 Othyan Byn, 10 Othyan Byn, 20 Othyan Byn, 50 Othyan Byn, 1 Othyan Tricone, 2 Othyan Tricones, and 5 Othyan Tricones.

The Othyan Tricones and Byn proportionally get larger the larger their value is, for example a 10 Othyan Tricones banknote is smaller than a 20 Othyan Tricones banknote, this makes it easier for blind people to use money. Additionally it automatically makes the coins worth more, as they get larger they have more silver in them, thus being worth more.

Othyan Byn = 1/100 Othyan Tricone

ECONOMY OF OTHYA

Main industries:
- Transportation equipment (road and rail)
- Communications equipment
- Textiles, foodstuffs, handicrafts
- Heavy machinery
- Aircraft
- Shipbuilding
- Glass making
- Complete range of mining (Mostly Coal, due to Othya's largest coal reserves in the world alongside Colandia)

Main imports:
- Lumber
- Vehicles, motor parts
- Medicine
- Scientific equipment

Main exports:
- High-grade coal
- Steel
- Textiles, foodstuffs, handicrafts
- Aircraft
- Ships
- Communications equipment

Currency: Othyan Tricone
Country Group: Developed, high income economy, natural-resource rich South Karemian economy with decreased reliance on foreign products
Population: 35,012,554 (1940)
Literacy rate: 79,66% (1940)
Economy type: Mixed Economy
Government: Constitutional monarchy
