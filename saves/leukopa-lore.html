# A WEEK OF TRAGEDY
July 6th - 13th, 1940:
It's been nearly 2 years since the Karemian military overthrew the military regime in control of Leukopan. Since then, High-ranking officials have been in and around the nation to assist the current government. Then it happened on June 6th, 1940. Leukopan Airlines flight 1075 a Hayakase, soon after take off from the Welnus International Airport in the nation's capital the aircraft underwent catastrophic hydraulic failure from what little radio signals remained from after the hydraulic failure it seems the pilots attempted to remain in control of the aircraft, however, an aircraft can't recover from such catastrophic hydraulic failure and crash into the ground at a high speed.

All but one of the 105 people aboard died. Among the dead were a Karemian Senators and their family, 5 high-ranking military officers, and all the civilians aboard. Aboard the plane was a member of the Karemian Royal Family, the only Karemian prince. Thankfully, he survived thanks to psionic capabilities, however, he is in critical condition and whether he survives is questionable at this point. Some of the best doctors in the world are currently working around the clock to make sure the young prince stays in the land of the living.

July 7th:
The Leukopan government launches an investigation into the Crash.

Families of the Victims demand compensation from Kiyonami and Leukopan Airlines. Leukopan Airlines agrees to pay once the reason for the crash has been identified.

July 13th:
While investigations will continue this is the report so far on the investigation into the crash:
First, several questions had to be answered:

Was it possible that Airbus is behind this somehow?
Some investigators believe that Airbus possibly sabotaged the Aircraft in an attempt to reverse their fortunes. However, after some investigation, this was proven to be impossible, for several reasons.
xzgamer55002/19/24
First of all, the company does not have the finances to currently fund such an undertaking. Second of all, most of those in charge of Airbus are technocrats obsessed with new technology and wouldn't simply blow something up.

Was improper maintenance behind this?
No, all maintenance procedures were followed correctly, and Leukopan ground crews have to undergo extensive training to obtain such a job.

Was the Pilot at fault?
Not impossible but extremely unlikely, it's very hard for someone to become a pilot in Leukopan because schools are extremely strict. Also considering the nature of what caused the crash, faulty piloting is extremely unlikely. Now this raises the question, could a better pilot have saved the plane? The answer is no, you simply can't recover an airplane after such catastrophic failure.

Now on to other notes.

An important discovery is that the Kiyonami aircraft seem to lack system redundancy, compared to Zoeing and Gong Airlines craft which have at least double redundant systems, and Airbus which has triple redundant systems.

Furthermore, there seem to be several flaws in airframe design, this is partly believed to be a result of the company rushing to develop the craft, possibly in a bid to get it to the market as soon as possible.

Some officials believe that Kiyonami sabotaged the airframes given to Leukopan Airlines in a bid to get back at Leukopan for the actions threatened the previous year.

However, much is still unknown and the investigation will continue, what is known currently will be published to the international community. Additionally, it's suggested that the planes be grounded until the investigation is solved.

In light of this new information families of the Victims begin suing Kiyonami.
