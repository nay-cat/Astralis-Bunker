# THE FIRST SOUTH KAREMIAN CONTINENTIAL WAR

Turn 1 in summary; (March 1836 - April 1836)

Norgei Front:

The bulk of the Xerian forces before the outbreak of the war had withdrawn from their northeastern border to strike the nations of Norgei and Tourego in prongs of four, the bulk of which fighting to secure Norgei. Indeed was it a brash surprise attack, that with the advantage of surprise and sheer force has been able to cover much land before the nations of Norgei and Tourego could consolidate a proportionate amount of forces. The Xerians struck Norgei on two prongs from the north, and from the Norgei port began marching out to encompass the nation's south. Luckily for the Norgeians; the Orau had coordinated an orderly retreat to a more defensible level of the country. The border guard of Norgei, though overwhelmed, took advantage of its rugged terrain to delay and frustrate the Xerian advance via mining the paths and felling trees along it. With obstruction in the mountains, such a massive force on the part of Xeria could not move as swiftly as they did initially, and in some parts stalled in their march.

This would allow the Continential forces tending to Norgei's north to mobilize and fortify upon their preferred lines with aid in manpower from the Norgei reserve; and once the Xerians painstakingly cut their way or otherwise circumvented these obstructions in a logistically plausible manner, their advance appeared to halt there for now, and could not continue without something to break it. Exchanges of fire from both sides are a constant here and for now will be the primary progenitor of casualties, and it is observed that both sides have a similar amount of men concurrently fighting.
